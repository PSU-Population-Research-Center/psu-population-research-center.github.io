# PRC Seminar Series

This guide summarizes the objectives and basic management of the seminar series. It will be reviewed annually to ensure it remains current. Feedback from attendees will be collected to help improve future seminars.

## Objectives of the Seminars

The PRC Seminar Series strengthens internal and external relationships by:

- **Enhancing Internal Interaction:** Enables PRC members to share updates on their work, improving awareness and coordination across the center’s project portfolio.
- **Fostering External Collaboration:** Strengthen connections with external partners by inviting guest researchers to present on topics relevant to the PRC.
- **Increasing Public Visibility:** Raise awareness of PRC’s research activities through regular updates and publications on public outlets.

Presentation topics should align with the center’s objectives and may include the following themes:

1. Population research: theory and methods
2. Oregon-related social science research
3. New policies affecting PRC’s mandate

Although PRC members may work on diverse topics, all members are expected to attend seminars and actively participate in discussions.

## Presentation Calendars

There is no define number of annual presentation and the frequency can be anything between every month to every 3 month. However the following requirement Must be meet and will define the number presentation per years

**Requirements:**

- Each PRC research staff member are required to present at least once per year.
- The Seminar Manager invites and schedules at 1 to 3 external speaker annually.
- Non-research staff are also encouraged to present and share information related to center administration.

**Annual Schedule:**

- The seminar calendar is planned and shared between July 1 and August 31 for the series starting in September.
- Regular sessions: September, October, November, February, March, May, June.
- Non-regular sessions may be scheduled for other months as needed.
- Special Session maybe orgainzed during the PRC annual retreat

**Notes:**

- April: excluded for PAA conference.
- July, August: summer break.
- December, January: end-of-year breaks.

## Seminar Manager Role

The Seminar Manager is responsible for:

- Establishing and maintaining the seminar presentation calendar, and communicating with presenters.
- Maintaining a public profile for the seminar series on major social networks.
- Seminars may be held jointly with other institutions within or outside PSU.

Presentations may use any format deemed suitable for the audience by the Seminar Manager. Basic Guidelines include:

- Sessions are online by default; in-person attendance if possible, maybe planned.
- Each session lasts 60 minutes, including remarks and discussion.

## Seminar Manager Tenure

- Tenure lasts one year, July 1 to June 30.
- Tenure may be renewed if approved by the PRC Director.
- The PRC Director designates the next Seminar Manager.
